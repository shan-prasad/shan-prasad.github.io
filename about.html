What are you studying?
I'm currently an undergraduate at UC¬†San Diego, studying Cognitive Science. My studies specialize in Interaction Design (IxD), focusing on how users interact with objects and how to make those interactions more simple, more accessible, and more meaningful.

Traditonally, IxD has been very focused on digital products, but as the world grows and new problems arise, a lot of my academic work aims to test how IxD principles and processes can apply beyond the digital world.
What do you love most about design?
I love that design is applicable to everything, even in sectors I wouldn't normally have expected them to be prevalent in.

I've had many opportunities I'm grateful for to collaborate with people in different fields and different specializations. It's been astonishing to see how my expertise merges with theirs and witness the collaborative output that creates impact that spans beyond just a deliverable.
How does entrepreneurship play a role in your work?
When I was a young and naive student, I expected projects to progress as smoothly as butter on marble. What I did learn within an entire segment of an incubator program, is that problems arise at every step of creating something valuable. There are no shortcuts to take if your ambitions are high for what you intend to output.

Entrepreneurship taught me that achieving anything comes with the need for three things: initiative, understanding, and execution. You've got to have the guts to try to output anything, you've got to understand the entire context of your situation, and you need a plan to carry out your vision.

This has made me an active person in the things I do, in my work, in managing others' work, and even in my social interactions. Entrepreneurship has made me widen my interests in what I involve myself in, but I still bring the work back to the knowledge I've grown.
